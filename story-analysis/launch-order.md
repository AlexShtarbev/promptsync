# Launch Order — Archives of the Forgotten

## Principles

- The pilot teaches the audience what the brand IS and how it works.
- Early episodes establish tonal range — prove the brand isn't one-note.
- Controversial episodes come after the brand has earned trust.
- Similar emotional registers (Sisyphus / Prometheus) must be spaced apart.

---

## Recommended Order

| Episode | Story | Why here |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **Pilot** | **Cassandra** or **Sisyphus** | Strongest mirror + cleanest reversal + lowest controversy risk. Cassandra has the edge on comment fuel and modern resonance. Sisyphus has the edge on visual simplicity. |
| **Ep 2-3** | **Orpheus**, **Minotaur** | Different emotional registers (self-sabotage, false heroism) that prove the brand isn't one-note. |
| **Ep 4-5** | **Judas**, **Prometheus** | By now the audience trusts the brand's tone. Judas drops as the controversy play when there's already a following to amplify it. Prometheus is spaced from Sisyphus. |
| **Ep 6+** | **Pandora**, then expand | The brand has a library. Each new episode benefits from the accumulated audience. |

---

## Pilot Decision: Cassandra vs. Sisyphus

### Cassandra as pilot
**For:** Highest comment fuel. Strongest modern resonance (every audience has their version). The "woman not believed" hook stops the scroll because it triggers personal recognition, not mythological curiosity. Proves from episode one that the brand is about recovering nerve, not retelling fairy tales.

**Against:** The pre-mortem risk (becoming a PSA) requires more disciplined execution. The story has more characters (Cassandra + the people who ignore her + the destruction that proves her right) which is a slightly harder AI-animation challenge.

### Sisyphus as pilot
**For:** One character, one object, one environment. The visual simplicity is ideal for establishing the brand's visual grammar without complicating it with multiple figures. The boulder is the most universally recognized symbol. Lowest execution risk.

**Against:** Lower comment fuel than Cassandra. The Camus association is a saturation risk — the audience may arrive thinking "I know this take." The pilot needs to feel fresh, and Sisyphus requires more precise execution to avoid the bumper-sticker trap.

## Series Expansion Candidates (beyond first 6)

After the first six episodes establish the brand, the candidate pool expands. Potential future episodes (not yet evaluated through full pre-mortem):

- **Abraham/Isaac** — the story is about Isaac, not Abraham (religious sensitivity, needs deep brand trust)
- **Oedipus** — his virtue caused the catastrophe (complex, may need 90s format)
- **Narcissus** — not vanity but inability to love (moderate reversal strength)
- **Achilles** — the choice, not the heel (may work at 90s with established audience patience)
- **Cain** — his offering was rejected without explanation (sympathy challenge in 60s)
- **Job** — God's answer wasn't an answer (abstract, may need 90s)
- **Lilith** — demanded equality, was cast out (recognition gap for general audience)

These require individual pre-mortem evaluation before greenlight.
